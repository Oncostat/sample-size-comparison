Welcome to this project dedicated to a focused comparison of computed sample sizes produced by East, nQuery, and R packages. This work looks specifically at the numerical results generated by each tool, placing them side by side to examine their consistency, differences, and overall behavior. Rather than exploring technical details on this page, the goal here is to offer a clear entry point into a structured comparison based purely on output values. I invite you to explore the results and discover how these tools align — or differ — when it comes to calculating sample size.

Motivations

Why R for sample size calculation ?

  • Do all analysis in R without having to use an external tool
  • Share code to someone (better than share an East of nQuery project)
  • Avoid problem with export
  • Free and open-source code, allows to open issue, ask new feature, fork the code
